EXTREMELY LOW-RESOURCE ASR FOR INDIAN LANGUAGES

INTRODUCTION

Development of domain-specific ASR systems and collection of labelled data for under-resourced Indian languages with little to nil amounts of accompanying labeled speech. Special focus on languages: Konkani, Maithili and Santali.

Konkani


2.2 million native speakers*

Indo-Aryan language

Spoken in: Karnataka, Maharashtra, Kerala, Gujarat and Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu

Maithili


7.3 million native speakers*

Indo-Aryan language

Spoken in: Bihar and Jharkhand as well as Nepal's eastern Terai

Santali


13.5 million native speakers*

Austroasiatic language

Spoken in: Assam, Bihar, Jharkhand, Mizoram, Odisha, Tripura and West Bengal

*2011 census

DATA

  • Speech data collected for all three languages in an interview style from native speakers resulting in more spontaneous speech

  • Investigate crowdsourcing for speech from native speakers via an Android app called CLAP (developed as part of an IMPRINT-2 grant)

  • Collection of 300 hours of transcribed conversational speech in each language

GOALS

Main goals



 
  1. Curated datasets consisting of around 300 hours of conversational speech in Konkani, Maithili and Santali
  2. Web APIs for domain-specific ASR in all three languages that serve as initial prototypes
  3. Android apps catering to each domain-specific ASR that will be further refined with user feedback

    4.  
     

Technical goals


  1. Evaluate on our target languages using existing pretrained multilingual models (e.g. XLSR)
  2. Develop adaptation techniques to finetune the pretrained models with labeled data in the target languages
  3. Develop self-supervision techniques that help the models perform better on conversational-style speech in our target languages
  4. Develop transfer learning approaches that help utilize conversational speech in one target language for the other target languages
